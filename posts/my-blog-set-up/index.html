I re-built my blog and have a quite comfortable workflow.

summary

A system built around Emacs

The main components on my system is deft and ox-hugo. Deft is an interface for my notes, blog-posts etc and ox-hugo is how I published them.

the integration of all my writing

All my notes are private, and they are totally uninhibited, I write about speculative stuff, taboo stuff, personal stuff and unfinished notes of science / philsophy / history and fiction. I also annotate and scrape stuff from the web. I use grasp due to the ease of setting it up but you might prefer using org-protcol-capture-html since it preserves the html formatting (I recall hypothes.is not being able to do that). I easily got it set up on my desktop linux system in like 10 minutes but for some reason I can’t on my laptop, I don’t know why?

I use edit-server.el to write comments and text on the web, add this snippet to your dot emacs to be able to save comments, this means everything I write on the internet is saved, nothing is lost, my notes, annotations, comments, blog-posts it is all saved on my computer, and better yet in one directory (~/private-notes), zip it (maybe also encrypt it) and upload it to the cloud for a simple back-up.

; https://aaron.baugher.biz/post/org-mode-saving-blog-comments/
(defun ajb/save-edit-server-buffer ()
  "Save the edit-server buffer as an org-mode entry via
   org-capture when it is saved with C-c C-c. Should be called
   from edit-server-done-hook."
  (when (not (string-match "edit-server" (buffer-name)))
    (goto-char (point-min))
    (insert (buffer-name) "\n")
    (mark-whole-buffer)
    (org-capture nil "e")
    (goto-char (point-min))
    (kill-line 1)))

(add-hook 'edit-server-done-hook 'ajb/save-edit-server-buffer)

You can also use org-journal if you want to store your diary as well.

So many blogs have basically only one real post where they talk about their blogging system, however since it is neatly integrated with notes, annotations, quotes and comments, I have in total probably ~500 pages+ of original writing. I assure you their will easily be more content.

the blogging packages

The 2 best blogging platforms that are best suited for my work flow is as mentioned ox-hugo and also org2blog, keep in mind however that you don’t have to monogamous on what you use, since I use org-mode and my workflow is so simple. It’s basically a matter of copy and paste if I wish to switch, probably easier then that.

The best feature of these packages is that your blog can be one file, this means you can have a holistic view of your blog and have it easily contained in your notes directory, I don’t have to share my private notes and make them blog-posts, I want to be able to share for example what I know to useful cybersecurity advice or X subject but not have to have hundreds of different files of private and public, that is not possible with 1 directory.

Currently I am using ox-hugo due to the fact that I want to use hugo. I think static site generators better fit my current use-case, in that I want free (in cost), customizability and speed.

conclusion

Please tell me a alternative blogging workflow that is able to do all of that. Emacs is the best tool for blogging.

Review of Books on Finance

posted on 2021-05-27

STILL A WORK IN PROGRESS

A survey of results on the topic, with a emphasis on the most practical. Note of course, these are just the ones I’ve read, there are obviously other amazing finance books but I’ve not read them.

the list is:

  • 4 Pillars of Investing (Bernstein 2002)
  • Ivy Portfolio (Faber 2009)
  • Global Asset Allocation (Faber 2015)
  • Simple Path to Wealth (Collin 2016)
  • Simple Path to Wealth (Collin 2016)
  • Index Funds: The 12-step Program for Active Investors (Hebner 2007)
  • Guru Investor (Forehand, Reese 2009)
  • The Misbehavior of Markets (Mandelbrot 2004)
  • Millionaire Next Door (Stanley 1996)
  • Early Retirement Extreme (Fisker 2010)
  • Tightwad Gazette (Dacyczyn 1992)

In regard to what is most important in understand and getting some benefits from finance I would say Mandelbrot if you want to understand the general field of finance and Faber if you want a good portfolio. All were valuable in my understanding of it.

4 pillars of investing

This is a classic book on finance that lays out all the essential details pretty well. I particularly like the section on risk and how it historically relates to different assets like ships during the age of exploration.

Ivy Portfolio

Very good book if you want to know how elite schools operate as well.
